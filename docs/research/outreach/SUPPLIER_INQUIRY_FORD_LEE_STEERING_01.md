Subject: Technical Data Request — 2020–2026 Ford F-450/F-550 Hydroboost Steering & Brake-Assist Hydraulic Requirements

Hello [Ford Fleet / Lee Power Steering / steering-specialist contact],

My company is engineering a Class 4/5 commercial-vehicle electrification prototype based on a Ford F-450/F-550 platform. Because the conversion removes the engine-driven power-steering pump, we must supply the hydroboost brake-assist circuit and the steering gear from an electric unit, and we need the factory hydraulic requirements to size and validate that replacement correctly.

We are not requesting certification approval or vehicle sign-off. We are requesting factory/reference hydraulic data so our engineering team can establish the exact requirements the replacement system must meet.

For a 2020–2026 Ford F-450/F-550 hydroboost steering system, could you please provide:

1. **Factory pump profile** — the flow-versus-RPM curve and maximum pressure-relief limit for the mechanical power-steering pump used on 2020–2026 F-450/F-550 trucks.

2. **Steering gear demand** — the peak flow (GPM) and pressure (PSI) required by the F-450/F-550 steering box during low-speed maneuvering at maximum front Gross Axle Weight Rating (GAWR).

3. **Hydroboost consumption rate** — the maximum fluid-volume displacement (cm³) required by the hydroboost cylinder during a sudden, fast panic-brake application.

4. **Return-line backpressure limit** — the maximum allowable backpressure (PSI) in the low-pressure return lines before it causes fluid to bypass internal seals or affects brake-pedal return.

5. **Accumulator energy capacity** — the exact internal volume and nitrogen-gas pre-charge pressure of the factory hydroboost accumulator cylinder used on Class 4/5 trucks (this determines the number of power-off emergency stops available if the electric pump fails).

If any of these are vehicle- or configuration-specific, we would be grateful for the applicable variants and the source document references. Thank you for your time.
