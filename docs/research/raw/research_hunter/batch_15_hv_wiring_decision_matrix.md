### Part 2: Upstream Engineering Dependencies
The architectural skeleton for the High-Voltage Wiring Package is fully mapped, and the decision rules are hard-coded. Downstream selection logic cannot advance past this point until the following fundamental question is answered by the human design authority:
**"What battery pack and inverter are we sizing this around?"**
Until these primary variables are explicitly populated, the following critical branches are blocked:
 1. **Conductor Sizing Strategy:** Cannot execute the trade-off calculation to determine if the chassis run demands a **35\text{ mm}^2 or 50\text{ mm}^2** shielded conductor envelope.
 2. **Terminal Contact Density:** Cannot select between an **180A or 250A** terminal size for the main traction bulkheads.
 3. **Overcurrent Coordination:** The exact structural ampacity rating and time-to-clear fault profile of the main fuse remains uncalculated.
 4. **Pre-Charge Loop Dynamics:** The exact resistance (R), continuous power rating, and adiabatic energy capacity (E in Joules) of the pre-charge network are locked from calculation.
